The Journey Begins: Our Educational Tour

March 30, & April 7, 2025

Day 0: Preparation and Departure

"This is it" I muttered as the whole batch of BS Information Technology graduat-waiting students strike a smile as the camera flashes for a group photo with our advisers who will be joining the tour. Finally, after months of preparation, we are off for deployment by April 07, 2025. The final orientation took place in the CCS building where our professors gave us an overview on what to expect, what to do, and what to bring during the tour.

After 1 week of waiting and preparing for my departure, the day finally came with good weather and atmosphere, but I actually fell ill a day before the tour, good thing I felt relieved after. Meanwhile, Monday of April 07, 2025 came with a very calm air. I hurried to the airport in the morning since my flight was scheduled at 8am. I arrived at the airport and got reumited with my fellow classmates and some professors whose flights were the same as mine. I could not have been more excited! It has been a long time since my last flight. But what is even more fun is that this is a once in a life-time experience where I get to travel with my classmates and we will be taking tours around some parts in manila! I am so looking forward to this day. Make sure to read the rest of the days, I am sure you will be having fun as I am!

Hello Manila!

April 07, 2025

Day 1: Rizal Park, Intramuros and Mall of Asia

Touchdown Manila! We linked up with our travel agency namely "DJM Travel and Tours". We hopped on their bus and secured a seat in the back, where I kind of felt dizzy from the flight. The bus finally took off, and there we first met our tour guide Sir Enan. He was so nice and you can tell he is very approachable. It took around 35 minutes until we finally arrived in our dormitory. From there I get to meet a few of my friends and classmates who arrived in Manila first. The accomodation in the dormitory was so ice, it's giving a home-like feels. However, it only felt like a 20minute rest, then our professors announced that we'll leave to proceed to the first itenirary - Rizal Park. Time skip, when we arrived, the place is scorching with very high heat temperature. But despite that, we get to see the huge Philippine flag and the statue of Jose Rizal. We took a short stop in a nearby icecream vendor to cool ourselves, then we continued to the next itenirary.

For the next itenerary: Intramuros, we went deep into the heart of Manila, the city is so modernized but the Intramuros has left remains from the past where it reminds us the story of our old capital went through. Sir Enan gave us a tour inside, from there we can old buildings, old forts, run-down walls, where you can really fell the vintage vibes of the past. Deep into the Intramuros, we finally arrived at the Fort Santiago the iconic memorial of the place. We took pictures, and Sir Enan explained the history of the fort. The tour inside the Intramuros ended at the Pasig river where we took lots of selfies because the view was mesmerizing. Finally, we exited from where we came from and we hopped back to the bus and surprisingly, we still have an extra time to go to the Mall! Our tourist guide said we can visit the Mall of Asia for 1 hour and 30mins which made us feel excited but clearly we know that ius not enough time to explore the famous MOA. When we arrived at the mall, me and my firends couldn't decide where to go so we explored to where we can possibly go, and we ended up in a photo studio, food court, and an open area. 1 hour and 30 minutes passed by so quickly so we went back to the meeting place of where the bus waited. Finally, when everyone found their way back, we headed back to the dormitory back in Quezon City. It took 45 mins to 1hour this time because of the heavy traffic. The day ended when we finally got our proper rest in the dorm, we took our baths, charged our phones, and prepared for tomorrow's early schedule.
